Frequently Asked Questions

Simply click on a question to view the answer!
General

If I remove a user, will the pupil data for that teacher disappear?

No, all assessment information is stored against a child and not against a user or a class.

I have mixed age classes, how can I do this in Learning Ladders?

When you import your pupil information from your management information system this will be taken care of for you. It is possible to have children of different ages in the same class. Please refer to our user guide section for more information on how to add a child to a class.

If I import my pupil information again, will this delete my assessment information?

No, Learning Ladders will simply update the information that has changed and disregard all other information. Pupil imports do not affect assessment information. If you have changed your class names, or structure, please be aware that this will default back to the classes within your Management Information System.

Why can't I log into Learning Ladders through the main Learning Ladders website?

As part of the Learning Ladders membership, each school gets their own personal URL which is hidden from all search engines. This is an extra security feature of Learning Ladders.

What should I do with my Year 6 leavers?

We recommend you update the name of these classes to include the academic year they left and then simply archive the class. This way you can retrieve their assessment data at a later date if required.
Assessment

What do the assessment points mean?

This depends on your assessment policy. Learning Ladders is designed to fit around the assessment policy for your school and what each assessment point means is a decision which needs to be made in school.

When should I award each assessment point?

This depends on your assessment policy. Learning Ladders is designed to fit around the assessment policy for your school and when an assessment point should be awarded is a decision which needs to be made in school.

Why do I need to set my pupil starting points as 'Start of Year'?

'Start of Year' simply means the pupils came into the year with this knowledge already. All historical assessment information should be noted as 'Start of Year'.

Does Age Related Expectation within Learning Ladders include Reception?

Yes, you will need to backfill for Reception. When creating your own content you will also need to create a set of rung statements for Reception.

Can I import my assessment data from another tracking system into Learning Ladders?

No. Due to the fact that Learning Ladders uses your school's personalised curriculum and not broad performance descriptors, we are unable to map your assessment data from other tracking systems into Learning Ladders.
Curriculum

I want to create my own Ladder content, how can I do this?

Great news! We recommend first checking out our user guides for changing your curriculum content. We'd also recommend changing your content right at the beginning of using Learning Ladders or in a new academic year.

Do I need to use the pupil booklets?

You don't need to use the booklets however, the booklets allow students to be involved in their own assessment and take ownership of their learning. If you have another method of doing this, please use this instead. Learning Ladders is designed to fit your school's assessment policy.
